Dystopian Fiction

Dystopian fiction describes imaginary societies where life is difficult or unfair. These stories are often set in the future and show worlds controlled by governments, technology or strict rules. Writers use dystopian fiction to criticise real problems in society.

Common themes in dystopian novels include lack of freedom, inequality, surveillance and rebellion. The main characters are usually young people who question the system and try to change it. These stories make readers think about the consequences of social and political decisions.

Popular dystopian novels include The Hunger Games and Divergent.
