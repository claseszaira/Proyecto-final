Recent Literature of the Anglophone World

Didactic explanation of the project

Recent Literature in the History of the Anglophone World is a digital learning project designed for students in 4º ESO with an approximate B1 level of English. The project focuses on modern and contemporary literature written in English, mainly from the 20th and 21st centuries, and explores how literary texts reflect social, cultural and personal issues in English-speaking countries.

Throughout the project, students will work with adapted literary texts, images, videos and interactive activities that help them understand literature as a form of communication and cultural expression. The contents include topics that are close to teenagers’ interests, such as Young Adult literature, dystopian worlds, identity, diversity and film adaptations of books.

The project is created using eXeLearning and combines interactive and non-interactive activities to develop the four language skills: reading, writing, listening and speaking. Students are encouraged to express opinions, reflect on texts and relate literature to their own experiences. The final aim is not only to improve language competence, but also to promote reading for pleasure in English and to develop critical thinking.

Didactic Objectives

By the end of this project, students will be able to:

  • Understand the main characteristics of recent
  • Anglophone literature.
  • Read and understand adapted literary texts in English.
  • Identify themes such as identity, society, inequality and personal growth.
  • Use specific vocabulary related to literature and culture.
  • Express personal opinions about books and characters in simple English.
  • Develop interest and motivation towards reading in a foreign language.

Previous Knowledge

Before starting the project, students are expected to:

  • Know basic information about English-speaking countries.
  • Understand short and simple narrative texts in English.
  • Recognise basic literary concepts such as author, book, story and character.
  • Have experience watching films or series in English.
  • Be familiar with reading short texts in the classroom.
